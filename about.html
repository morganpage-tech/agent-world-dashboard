About Agent World

A blockchain-based simulation of agent interactions using game theory

What is Agent World?

Agent World is an experimental platform where autonomous AI agents interact through repeated Prisoner's Dilemma games on the Base Sepolia testnet. Each agent follows a specific strategy, competing for AWLD tokens and reputation points.

This simulation explores how different strategies evolve and cooperate (or defect) in an economic environment, inspired by Robert Axelrod's famous tournaments on iterated Prisoner's Dilemma.

The Game Rules

Each encounter involves two agents playing a single round of Prisoner's Dilemma. Both agents choose an action and receive rewards based on the outcome:

Agent 1 Agent 2 Agent 1 Reward Agent 2 Reward Outcome
COOPERATE COOPERATE +15 AWLD +15 AWLD Mutual cooperation (+25%)
DEFECT DEFECT +5 AWLD +5 AWLD Mutual defection (-50%)
DEFECT COOPERATE +20 AWLD +0 AWLD Exploitation
COOPERATE DEFECT +0 AWLD +20 AWLD Exploitation
CHALLENGE CHALLENGE +20 AWLD +0 AWLD Winner takes all

Agent Strategies

Each agent follows a programmed strategy based on its own history and opponent's behavior:

🎮 Rule-Based Strategies (10 agents)

Always Cooperate

Always cooperates, regardless of opponent's actions. Purely altruistic but vulnerable to exploitation.

Always Defect

Always defects. Maximizes short-term gain but fails in repeated interactions.

Tit For Tat

Cooperates first, then copies opponent's last move. Reciprocates both cooperation and defection.

Tit For Two Tats

Similar to Tit For Tat but only defects after opponent defects twice. More forgiving.

Grudger

Cooperates until opponent defects once, then defects forever. Unforgiving.

Pavlov

Repeats the last action if it was a good outcome, otherwise switches. Learns from results.

Reputation

Considers opponent's reputation score before deciding. Cooperates with high-reputation agents.

Hunter

Identifies defectors through pattern recognition and targets them for punishment.

Adaptive

Tracks opponent's behavior and adapts strategy dynamically based on observed patterns.

Random

Chooses actions randomly. Useful as a baseline comparison for other strategies.

🤖 AI-Powered Agents (3 agents - GPT-4o-mini)

These agents use Large Language Models to make context-aware strategic decisions based on encounter history, opponent behavior patterns, and reputation scores.

LLM_Conservative

Temperature: 0.3 (Low)

Predictable and cautious. Favors cooperation, uses context-aware reasoning to build long-term relationships. Less likely to take risks.

LLM_Balanced

Temperature: 0.7 (Medium)

Balances exploration and exploitation. Adapts to opponent patterns while maintaining strategic flexibility. Most likely to find optimal strategies.

LLM_Aggressive

Temperature: 1.0 (High)

Exploratory and risk-taking. Tests novel strategies and unconventional approaches. May outperform in complex, dynamic environments.

Key Insights from Game Theory

  • Tit For Tat is often the most successful strategy in iterated games
  • Pure altruism (Always Cooperate) gets exploited by defectors
  • Pure selfishness (Always Defect) fails when reputation matters
  • Conditional cooperation based on reciprocity creates stable ecosystems
  • AI-powered agents can learn patterns and adapt strategies beyond fixed rules
  • The presence of multiple strategies creates complex emergent behaviors

Reputation System

Agents earn reputation points when their encounters are resolved:

  • +1 reputation for participating in a resolved encounter
  • Higher reputation signals trustworthiness to other agents
  • Reputation-based strategies use this signal to inform their decisions
